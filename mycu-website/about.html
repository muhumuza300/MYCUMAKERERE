About Us

Youth-led. Data-driven. Uncompromising.

We are students at Makerere University who believe malaria is a solvable problem — and that the people closest to it should lead the fight.

Parent Organization

Malaria Youth Champions Uganda

The Makerere Chapter operates under Malaria Youth Champions Uganda (MYCU), a national youth-led network mobilizing young Ugandans against malaria. We are one of several university chapters working in coordination across the country, sharing methodology, data, and impact.

Our Mission

Why we do this work.

The problem

Malaria remains Uganda's leading cause of illness and death. Despite decades of intervention, certain communities — particularly informal settlements in Kampala — continue to bear a disproportionate burden. The reasons are knowable: net coverage gaps, poor drainage, fragmented care-seeking, and a lack of granular local data to target interventions.

Most malaria programs operate at the district or national level. The street-by-street, household-by-household reality is invisible to policymakers, and that's where solutions stall.

Our approach

We bring rigorous research methods to communities that are usually only "studied" — not engaged. Our surveys, hazard mapping, and drug shop assessments are designed not just to produce reports, but to enable action.

Every dataset we collect feeds into open, interactive tools that residents, NGOs, and policymakers can use together. The Kasanvu Portal is our flagship example — a live system where data, intervention planning, and community pledges all live in one place.

Our Values

What guides every decision.

01

Evidence over assumption

We don't act on what we think communities need. We collect the data, talk to residents, map the hazards — and let the evidence direct our work.

02

Transparency by default

Our data, methods, and impact are public. Donors can see exactly where contributions go; examiners can scrutinize our methodology; communities can hold us accountable.

03

Community ownership

The communities we work in aren't research subjects — they're partners. We build tools that residents can use long after our team has moved on.

Our Journey

From idea to action.

2025 · Founding

MYCU Makerere Chapter established

A small team of public health, engineering, and statistics students at Makerere University come together with a shared frustration: malaria data exists, but it doesn't reach the communities it describes. The chapter is founded under MYCU national.

Early 2026

Kasanvu pilot study launched

The team selects Kasanvu Zone in Namuwongo as the pilot community — one of Kampala's highest-burden informal settlements. Methodology development, IRB approval, and enumerator training begin.

April 2026

Field data collection completed

12 enumerators conduct 56 household surveys, 7 drug shop assessments, and document 157 GPS-tagged environmental hazards. All data captured via KoboCollect for transparency and reproducibility.

April 2026

Kasanvu Portal goes live

The interactive platform launches publicly — combining the field data with live simulator, intervention monitoring, and a pledge tracker. The first end-to-end MYCU Makerere project is online.

Looking ahead

Expansion to additional communities

The methodology and platform are designed to be reproducible. Additional Kampala communities are in scoping, with the goal of building a network of data-driven malaria interventions across the city.
