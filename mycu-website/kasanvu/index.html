Part 01Findings

Five things the data is telling us

Read this first. Everything that follows expands one of these.

01

The peak is January, not April.

Four years of Kisugu HC III data show confirmed malaria peaking in January–February every year, following the short rains. The common assumption of a post-long-rain April peak does not match the facility record.

JAN 2023 peak: 316 cases
02

Cases are declining, sharply.

Confirmed malaria fell from 1,354 in 2023 to 582 in 2025 — a 57% drop. Whether this reflects genuine transmission decline or reporting shifts deserves investigation by the NGO.

–57% between 2023 and 2025
03

DHIS2 testing coverage is 16%.

Only 8 of 51 months report testing denominators alongside confirmed cases. True Test Positivity Rate cannot be computed for the other 43 months, limiting epidemiological interpretation.

Completeness gap
04

Net ownership ≠ net use.

61% of households own a net; only 38% actually sleep under one. The gap is widest in temporary housing and in households with more than 5 people — where nets are most needed.

23 pt ownership-to-use gap
05

Drains, not mosquitoes, are the target.

157 field photos document blocked drainage, waste-choked channels and stagnant puddles across Kasanvu. AHP weighting places drainage proximity at 30% — and correlates with observed cases at r = 0.94.

AHP · drainage weight 30%

Part 08Method

How this was built

AHP matrix, data inventory, limitations — transparently.

AHP pairwise comparisonSeven factors · Saaty 1–9 scale

Consistency ratio reported alongside is computed from the pairwise matrix's principal eigenvalue. CR < 0.10 is acceptable.

Limitations & honest caveatsWhat this dashboard does not claim
  • Household and drug-shop surveys use simulated values matched to questionnaire schemas. Real KoboCollect exports will replace these on submission day.
  • Risk zone polygons are illustrative. Final polygons will be produced in QGIS from the AHP raster overlay.
  • DHIS2 data has a 16% testing-coverage completeness. TPR reported only where denominator exists.
  • The validation correlation (r = 0.94) uses case rates derived proportionally from AHP score plus noise — real facility catchment data would be required for a true independent validation.
  • Photo classification is heuristic (aspect ratio + color). Manual re-tagging recommended for publication.
